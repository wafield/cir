The community issue is presented as follows:

Issue description

One goal of the borough is to maintain quality of life. A proposal to address quality of life is to increase property taxes each year in line with inflation. Property tax affects everyone in the borough and is one of the only two ways that the borough can generate revenue (one being property taxes, another being wage taxes).

Should the borough maintain or increase property tax in line with inflation to address quality of life issues such as property crime?

Proposed action

The proposal is for the State College Borough government to implement an annual automatic inflation-based property tax increase. The Borough expects its annual costs of operation to increase by at least the rate of inflation, just as every consumer and business experience price increases. The index used to compute the annual property tax increase would be based on the Municipal Cost Index (MCI), a measure of the increase in the cost of goods and services typically procured by municipalities.
